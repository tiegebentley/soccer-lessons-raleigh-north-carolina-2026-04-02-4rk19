Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our summer soccer training programs in Raleigh, NC.

What age groups do you train?

We offer programs for youth players ages 6-17, with groups organized by age and skill level. Our youngest groups (ages 6-8) focus on fundamental ball skills and fun introduction to the game, while older groups progress to advanced technical training, tactical understanding, and position-specific development. We assess each player to ensure they're placed in the appropriate group for their abilities.

How long are the training sessions?

We offer both week-long summer camps (Monday-Friday, 2-3 hours per day) and ongoing weekly training sessions throughout the summer. Full-day camp options are also available for working parents. Each session is carefully structured to maximize learning while preventing fatigue and burnout. Training includes warm-ups, technical drills, small-sided games, and cool-down periods.

What if my child is a beginner?

Perfect! We welcome players of all skill levels, from complete beginners to competitive players. Our coaches are experienced in working with new players and creating a supportive, encouraging environment. Beginners are grouped with players of similar experience, allowing them to learn at an appropriate pace without feeling overwhelmed. We focus on building confidence, fundamental skills, and a love for the game.

How much does the program cost?

Program costs vary depending on the session type, duration, and intensity level. Week-long camps, ongoing weekly sessions, and private lessons each have different pricing structures. We offer early registration discounts and sibling discounts to make our programs more affordable. Contact us for detailed pricing information and current promotions. We believe in transparent pricing with no hidden fees.

What should my child bring to training?

Players should bring a properly sized soccer ball, shin guards (required), cleats or turf shoes, athletic clothing appropriate for outdoor activity, a water bottle (hydration is crucial!), and sunscreen. We recommend bringing a backup shirt and extra water for hot summer days. A small snack for longer sessions is also helpful. All equipment should be clearly labeled with your child's name.
